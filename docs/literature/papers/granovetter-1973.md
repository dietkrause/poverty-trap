# Granovetter (1973)

**The Strength of Weak Ties.** *American Journal of Sociology*, 78(6),
1360-1380.

- DOI: https://doi.org/10.1086/225469
- Widely available author copies and JSTOR access.

## What it shows (in our words)

Weak ties (acquaintances, not close friends) are disproportionately valuable for
spreading information across otherwise separate social clusters. Crucially, many
people find jobs and opportunities through weak ties rather than strong ones,
because weak ties bridge to parts of the network you cannot otherwise reach.

## What we borrow

That access to opportunity flows through network structure, and that bridging
ties matter more than sheer number of contacts.

## How it maps to the model

It motivates linking opportunity arrival to the network in section 7.5-7.6: an
agent's opportunity-arrival rate rises with connectedness, and the visualisation
plan distinguishes bridging (cross-band) ties from within-band ties. It is the
mechanism behind "move to a connected neighbourhood and doors open."
