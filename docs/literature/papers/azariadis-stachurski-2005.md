# Azariadis & Stachurski (2005)

**Poverty Traps.** In *Handbook of Economic Growth*, vol. 1A, ch. 5. Elsevier.

- DOI: https://doi.org/10.1016/S1574-0684(05)01005-1
- A freely available author/working-paper version circulates; link it from the
  authors' pages. The note below is our own summary.

## What it shows (in our words)

The canonical synthesis of poverty-trap theory. It formalises self-reinforcing
mechanisms (credit constraints, thresholds, coordination failures) as dynamical
systems whose accumulation map `k_{t+1} = f(k_t)` is S-shaped and so admits
multiple equilibria: a low trap and a high steady state separated by an unstable
threshold.

## What we borrow

The dynamical-systems framing: a single transition map with two stable
attractors and one unstable separatrix, and the idea that history / initial
conditions select which attractor you reach.

## How it maps to the model

It underpins the bistable view in section 7: structure shapes the map so the
low-wealth region is its own basin. Our model adds heterogeneity (per-agent
talent, effort, shocks) and the stochastic crossing probability, but the
skeleton is this S-shaped map.
