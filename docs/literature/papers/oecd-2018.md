# OECD (2018)

**A Broken Social Elevator? How to Promote Social Mobility.** OECD Publishing,
Paris.

- DOI: https://doi.org/10.1787/9789264301085-en
- OECD provides open summaries and figures; link from the official page.

## What it shows (in our words)

A cross-country report on social mobility. A widely cited headline estimate is
the number of generations it would take a child from a low-income family to
reach the average income in their country - several generations in many places,
and notably more where mobility is low. It documents how education, health, and
neighbourhood reinforce immobility.

## What we borrow

A concrete, recognisable yardstick: "generations to reach mean income."

## How it maps to the model

It is a second **calibration target** for the generational layer (section 7.8),
complementary to the IGE: we can run lineages and measure how many generations
they take to reach the population mean, and compare to the OECD figure for a
given country. It also supports the policy-regime story (section 7.9), since the
report ties mobility to public services.
