# Mani, Mullainathan, Shafir & Zhao (2013)

**Poverty Impedes Cognitive Function.** *Science*, 341(6149), 976-980.

- DOI: https://doi.org/10.1126/science.1238041
- Free author copies circulate (Harvard Kennedy School / Princeton faculty
  pages). The note below is our own summary.

## What it shows (in our words)

Across lab and field studies, the authors find that the mental load of poverty
itself reduces available cognitive bandwidth. When people are preoccupied with
scarcity, performance on reasoning tasks drops, comparable to a meaningful loss
of effective cognitive capacity. The effect is situational, not a trait of poor
people: the same person performs worse when financially stressed.

## What we borrow

That scarcity *taxes* cognition, so the same effort converts into less value when
you are poor or burdened.

## How it maps to the model

It is the empirical basis for the **effort efficiency** `eta` in section 7.2: a
multiplier in `[eta_min, 1]` that falls below the poverty line and with the
stressor load, degrading the value-creation term. It is why two agents with
identical effort end up in different places.
