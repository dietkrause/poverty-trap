# Ghatak (2015)

**Theories of Poverty Traps and Anti-Poverty Policies.** *The World Bank
Economic Review*, 29(suppl_1), S77-S105.

- DOI: https://doi.org/10.1093/wber/lhv013
- Free (LSE author PDF): https://personal.lse.ac.uk/ghatak/povertytraps.pdf

## What it shows (in our words)

A theoretical map of why poverty persists, distinguishing individual-level traps
(thresholds in human or physical capital, behavioural channels) from
collective-level ones (institutions, markets). A recurring mechanism is that
credit-market failures and the lack of a safety net make adverse events far more
costly for the poor.

## What we borrow

The idea that being poor is structurally *more expensive*: the same shock costs a
poor household more (a "poverty premium").

## How it maps to the model

It is the basis for the **poverty premium** term `pi_pov(w)` in section 7.4 (an
extra negative drift below the poverty line) and for making the diffusion shocks
asymmetric (larger volatility for the poor). It also motivates the welfare-floor
lever in the regime layer (section 7.9).
