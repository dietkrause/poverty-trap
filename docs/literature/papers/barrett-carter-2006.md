# Barrett & Carter (2006)

**The Economics of Poverty Traps and Persistent Poverty: Empirical and Policy
Implications** (asset-based approach). *Journal of Development Studies*, 42(2),
178-199.

- DOI: https://doi.org/10.1080/00220380500405261
- Paywalled at the publisher; search the authors' university pages for an open
  working-paper version. The note below is our own summary.

## What it shows (in our words)

Poverty can persist because of an asset threshold. The relationship between
assets today and assets tomorrow is S-shaped, crossing the break-even line three
times: a low stable equilibrium, a high stable equilibrium, and an unstable
point between them. Households below the unstable point slide down; those above
it accumulate. The unstable point is the **Micawber threshold**.

## What we borrow

The existence of a single critical threshold separating two basins of
attraction, and that small gains below it do not help unless they push you past
it.

## How it maps to the model

It is the conceptual basis for the upper escape barrier `w*` (the "became rich"
wall) and, more deeply, for treating escape as a basin-crossing problem. Our
drift-diffusion is the stochastic version: structure makes drift negative below
the line, so crossing requires either luck or sustained effort.
